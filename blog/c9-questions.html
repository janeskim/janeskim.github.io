On Asking Questions


Learning to code has presented challenges at every turn: technical concepts have been hard to grasp at first glance and it's usually through hours of trial and error that I am able to code a working solution. As a result, learning has required resourcefulness. Thankfully there's a lot of documentation available on the web: forums such as stackoverflow and stackexchange are terrific places for exchanging knowledge and I've learned a lot about how to solve various programming issues through these sites.

Because of the mass of information out there, it's important to parse it wisely -- a big part of that entails in asking questions the right way. Well-written questions are specific, informative, and concise.

Many questions have already been asked and answered, and specific questions demonstrate how the situation at hand is different from the ones already on the site. Informative questions are easy to read and understand: they always explain the attempted solution and the resulting problem, and they provide context such as the web language and platform. Informative questions also include information such as error messages and snippets of relevant code. Lastly, good questions need to be concise. It allows readers to scan through your question and quickly understand the issue at hand. A good title improves the chances for your question to be answered.
